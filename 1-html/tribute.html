All Blues

Miles Davis was, and for some still is, one of the most influential musicians of the 20th Century. He paved the way for modern jazz musicians as well as penning some of the most recognizable jazz standards. Milestones (1958) and Kind of Blue (1959) are a pair of the most popular jazz albums of all time. During his career, he was nominated for and won multiple awards--including 32 Grammy nominations with eight wins and he was inducted into the Rock-n-Roll Hall of Fame in March 2006 (15 years posthumously).
